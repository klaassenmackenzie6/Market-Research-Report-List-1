<p><p>The cellulose derivative excipient market comprises three main types: cellulose ether derivatives, cellulose ester derivatives, and others. Cellulose ether derivatives, such as hydroxypropyl methylcellulose (HPMC) and carboxymethyl cellulose (CMC), are widely used for their thickening and binding properties in pharmaceuticals and food. Cellulose ester derivatives, including acetyl cellulose and cellulose acetate, are valued for their film-forming and drug-release control capabilities. Other derivatives may include specialized forms like microcrystalline cellulose, catering to diverse applications in industries such as cosmetics and packaging.</p></p>
