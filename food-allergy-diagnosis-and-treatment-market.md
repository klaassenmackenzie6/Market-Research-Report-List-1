<p><strong>The Food Allergy Diagnosis and Treatment Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Skin Prick Test</li><li>Blood Test</li><li>Food Elimination Test</li><li>Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)</li><li>Enzyme-linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Food allergy diagnosis and treatment encompass various market types aimed at identifying and managing allergies. The skin prick test involves applying allergens to the skin to observe reactions. Blood tests measure specific IgE antibodies. Food elimination tests entail removing suspected allergens from the diet to identify triggers. Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) detects genetic material of allergens, while enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) quantifies allergen-specific antibodies in the blood. Additional methods include patch tests and food challenges, broadening diagnostic approaches.</p></p>
