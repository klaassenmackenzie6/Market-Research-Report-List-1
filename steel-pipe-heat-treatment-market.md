<p><p>The steel pipe heat treatment market includes several types, each serving specific purposes. Hardening enhances strength by increasing hardness through controlled heating and cooling. Tempering follows hardening to reduce brittleness while retaining toughness, making pipes more durable. Quenching rapidly cools the steel, locking in desirable properties for improved performance. Other treatments may involve processes like annealing or normalizing, aimed at achieving specific mechanical characteristics. Collectively, these methods are crucial for optimizing steel pipe performance in various industrial applications.</p></p>
