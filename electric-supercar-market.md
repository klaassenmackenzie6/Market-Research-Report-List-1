<p><p>The Electric Supercar market includes Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs), which rely solely on electric power stored in batteries, offering zero emissions and high performance. Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) combine electric motors with internal combustion engines, allowing short electric-only drives and extended range with fuel. Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs) use both electric motors and combustion engines but cannot be plugged in, relying on regenerative braking and engine power for charging. Each type caters to varying performance, efficiency, and sustainability preferences in the luxury supercar segment.</p></p>
