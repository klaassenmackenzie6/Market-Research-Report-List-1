<p><p>The antimalarial drug market is segmented based on the malaria-causing Plasmodium species. **Plasmodium falciparum** is the most severe and prevalent, driving demand for potent drugs like artemisinin-based therapies. **Plasmodium vivax** requires treatments targeting dormant liver stages, such as primaquine. **Plasmodium malariae** causes milder symptoms, often treated with chloroquine. **Plasmodium ovale** is rare and resembles P. vivax, requiring similar liver-stage treatments. Each species' unique biology and resistance patterns shape the development and distribution of targeted antimalarial drugs globally.</p></p>
