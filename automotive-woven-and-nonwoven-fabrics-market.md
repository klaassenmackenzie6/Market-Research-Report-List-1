<p><p>The automotive fabrics market is divided into two main types: woven and nonwoven fabrics. Woven fabrics are crafted by interlacing yarns, providing durability and aesthetic appeal, often used for seat covers and interiors. Nonwoven fabrics, on the other hand, are made by bonding fibers through mechanical, thermal, or chemical processes, offering benefits like lightweight, moisture resistance, and sound insulation. Both fabric types contribute significantly to vehicle performance, comfort, and aesthetics, catering to diverse automotive applications and consumer preferences.</p></p>
