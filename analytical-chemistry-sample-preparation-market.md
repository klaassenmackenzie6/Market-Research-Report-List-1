<p><p>Analytical chemistry sample preparation encompasses various techniques critical for accurate analysis. Cutting and slicing involve physically sectioning samples for examination. Fixation preserves biological specimens, maintaining cellular structure. Embedding involves encasing samples in a medium for stability during analysis. Coating, often with substances like gold, enhances imaging quality in electron microscopy. Immuno-gold labeling employs gold nanoparticles to visualize specific proteins in biological samples. Each method ensures that samples are adequately prepared to yield reliable and precise analytical results.</p></p>
